## Summary
The Central Provident Fund (CPF) Board's annual report for 2023 highlights the organization's efforts to support Singaporeans in achieving a secure retirement. The report emphasizes the importance of financial planning and literacy, particularly among younger members. To this end, the CPF Board collaborated with MoneySense on a retirement planning campaign and introduced the Year-in-Review (YIR) statement to provide personalized insights to members.

The report also highlights the organization's commitment to sustainability, with a 21% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions and meeting targets for energy and water efficiency. Proposed initiatives include achieving Green Mark certification and adopting district cooling systems.

In terms of supporting Singaporeans' retirement adequacy, the CPF system aims to ensure sufficient savings throughout working years. Initiatives introduced in 2023 included additional support for seniors and lower-wage workers.

Key metrics and targets mentioned in the report include:

* 21% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions
* 10% reduction targets for Energy Utilisation Index (EUI) and Water Efficiency Index (WEI)
* Achieving net zero before 2045
* $3 billion in cash received by 2.9 million Singapore Citizens as part of the Assurance Package
* $302 million received by 1.9 million Singapore Citizens in their MediSave Account

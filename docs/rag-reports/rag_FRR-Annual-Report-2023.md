## Summary
The Fonds de Réserve pour les Retraites (FRR) annual report for 2023 highlights the organization's commitment to sustainable finance disclosures. The report includes a foreword by the President of the Supervisory Board and the Executive Board, as well as various sections detailing the FRR's strategy, financial results, and non-financial performance.

Key metrics include:

* A 5% reallocation of non-hedged equities to high-yield bonds
* A 112.8% increase in returns on assets since 2010, compared to a 21.8% increase for hedging assets
* Operating income of €26 million in 2023, primarily from external services
* Financial income of €2.6 billion in 2023, driven by income on disposal of financial instruments and foreign exchange gains

The report also highlights the FRR's approach to risk management and control, including its incorporation of climate change scenarios into its macroeconomic and financial assumptions.

In terms of targets and actions, the report notes that:

* Managers must provide qualitative reports focused on ESG aspects twice a year
* The FRR requires reporting on GHG emissions levels for portfolios and benchmarks
* Managers must favour companies whose strategy is in line with the Paris Agreement
